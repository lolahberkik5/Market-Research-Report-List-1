<p><p>The Wide Domain Automotive Oxygen Sensor market includes primarily two types: Titanium Oxide and Zirconia sensors. Titanium Oxide sensors operate on resistive principles, reacting to changes in the oxygen levels by altering their resistance, making them suitable for low-temperature applications. In contrast, Zirconia sensors utilize a solid electrolyte made of zirconium dioxide, functioning effectively at higher temperatures by generating a voltage based on the oxygen concentration. Both types play vital roles in optimizing engine performance and reducing emissions in modern vehicles.</p></p>
