<p><ul><li>Mechanical Lidar</li><li>Solid State Lidar</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive Lidar system market primarily comprises two types: mechanical Lidar and solid-state Lidar. Mechanical Lidar employs rotating components to scan the environment, offering high-resolution 3D mapping but usually at a higher cost and complexity. In contrast, solid-state Lidar lacks moving parts, making it more compact, durable, and cost-effective, which is advantageous for mass adoption in vehicles. Both technologies play crucial roles in enhancing autonomous driving capabilities by providing accurate distance measurements and obstacle detection.</p></p>
