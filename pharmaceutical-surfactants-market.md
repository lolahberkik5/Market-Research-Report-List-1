<p><p>The pharmaceutical surfactants market is categorized into several types based on their charge properties. Anionic surfactants are negatively charged and effective in solubilizing drugs. Cationic surfactants, positively charged, often possess antimicrobial properties, aiding in preservation. Amphoteric surfactants exhibit both positive and negative charges, providing versatility in formulations. Nonionic surfactants are neutral, favored for their mildness and compatibility, often used in sensitive applications. Other surfactants may include various specialized compounds, catering to unique formulation needs in pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
