<p><p>Ternary precursor markets are categorized mainly into NCM type and NCA type, which relate to the production of lithium-ion battery materials. The NCM (nickel-cobalt-manganese) type emphasizes a balanced mix of these metals for enhanced energy density and stability, making it suitable for high-performance applications. In contrast, the NCA (nickel-cobalt-aluminum) type focuses on improved thermal stability and lifecycle, often used in high-energy applications like electric vehicles. Each type serves distinct requirements in battery technology, influencing market dynamics and product development.</p></p>
