<p><p>Crystalline fructose is a highly pure form of fructose derived from starch or sucrose hydrolysis. In starch hydrolysis, enzymes break down starch into glucose, which is then converted into fructose, ideal for various pharmaceutical applications due to its sweetness and low glycemic index. Conversely, sucrose hydrolysis involves the enzymatic cleavage of sucrose into glucose and fructose. Both processes cater to the pharmaceutical market's demand for sweeteners, excipients, and formulations enhancing drug delivery and patient compliance.</p></p>
