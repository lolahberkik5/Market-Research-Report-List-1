<p><p>The large hydro turbine runner market comprises several types, each designed for specific operational conditions. Francis turbines are versatile and suitable for medium-head applications, making them popular in many hydroelectric plants. Kaplan turbines excel in low-head environments, utilizing adjustable blades for efficiency. Pelton turbines are designed for high-head applications, featuring spoon-shaped buckets that harness kinetic energy from fast-moving water jets. Other turbine types may include bulb and cross-flow designs, catering to diverse hydroelectric needs and optimizing energy extraction across various settings.</p></p>
